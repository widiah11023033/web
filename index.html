Recreational Therapy

Recreational therapy is a type of therapy that uses leisure and leisure activities to promote physical, mental and emotional well-being. It can be a useful addition to the rehabilitation process for clients currently undergoing rehabilitation.

Activity Programs

Outside Activities

Running, hiking, glamping, martial arts, water sports, air sports, equestrian sports, archery, target shooting, Balinese cultural performances, nature exploration, fishing, ATV riding, and stargazing sessions.

Art Activities

Painting, sculpting, silver ring making, Balinese handicrafts, traditional woven fabric creation, and Balinese mask making - all tailored to individual preferences and therapeutic needs.

The Benefits of Recreational Therapy

Improving Physical and Mental Well-being

Recreational therapy helps improve physical fitness and mental health, reducing stress, anxiety, and depression while increasing feelings of joy and happiness.

Building Social Support

Group activities foster social connections and build a sense of community among individuals in rehabilitation, creating positive relationships with others who share similar experiences.

Positive Emotions

Provides alternative and healthier ways to deal with emotions through sports, arts, music, and outdoor activities, helping process feelings in positive and constructive ways.

Increase Self-Esteem and Confidence

Participation helps individuals regain a sense of accomplishment, set and achieve goals, experience success, and learn new skills, contributing to positive self-image.

Reducing Desire and Risk of Recurrence

Offers fun and satisfying alternatives without substance use, helping reduce cravings and recurrence risk by providing pleasure and satisfaction through healthy activities.

Improve Cognitive Function

Activities improve cognitive functions such as memory, problem-solving, and decision-making through mentally stimulating exercises that restore abilities impaired by substance use.

Teach Stress Management

Teaches healthier ways to deal with stress, boredom, and triggers, helping develop long-term coping strategies that enhance sobriety and overall well-being.

Providing Purpose

Gives clients a sense of purpose and structure, helping establish daily routines, set goals, and feel accomplishment, cultivating motivation and positive mindset for recovery.

Increase Mindfulness and Self Awareness

Focuses on being present and cultivating mindfulness, helping individuals become more self-aware, understand triggers and patterns, and develop healthier thinking and behavior.
